Data

The model turns ordinary cafeteria counts into operational timing.

No student names. No cameras. No private records. Just aggregate cafeteria patterns and a narrow question: what will return unopened, and what can still be safely rescued?

Architecture

01 / Signals

Raw cafeteria context becomes a live operating picture.

Menu, weather, events, attendance, line placement, cooler capacity, and after-school demand enter the system as aggregate signals.

02 / Models

The AI separates normal lunch noise from ghost-component risk.

Classifier, quantile forecasters, demand matcher, and anomaly detector turn routine counts into explainable predictions.

03 / Gates

Recommendations pass through food-safety constraints.

The state machine blocks unsafe reuse, flags cold-chain requirements, and keeps approval with the cafeteria manager.

04 / Action

The output is not a chart. It is a timed decision.

Deploy the cooler, stage in waves, move line placement, route sealed surplus, or block the action and log why.

Food waste track

Served-but-unwanted food is a hidden school climate lever.

Pattern detection plus recommendations.

Classifier, quantile forecasts, demand matching, anomaly detection, and a rule-constrained action scorer.

Keep food feeding students.

Second Bell prioritizes prevention, share-table recovery, and same-day approved routing before disposal.

Cold items need stricter gates.

Sealed packaging, monitored cooler capacity, and temperature assumptions are visible in every counted action.

Surplus meets real same-day demand.

Tutoring, robotics, band, and track can use eligible sealed items without outside donation logistics.

The AI does not approve food safety.

Human approval is not decoration. It is the control that keeps the system realistic and responsible.
