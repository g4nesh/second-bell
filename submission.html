Project description

A narrow, recoverable waste mechanism.

Most food-waste tools treat a cafeteria like one big trash stream. Second Bell focuses on unopened school-lunch items that count as served but come back untouched. The system predicts what is likely to return, explains why today is risky, and recommends a timed action before the safe recovery window closes.

AI architecture

Inputs become a production inference array.

Feature encoder

Twenty-two aggregate cafeteria features are encoded from menu, period, attendance, weather, event, line setup, cooler capacity, and demand.

Prediction heads

RandomForestClassifier scores ghost risk; quantile GradientBoostingRegressor models produce Q10/Q50/Q90 return bands.

Demand + anomaly

RandomForestRegressor estimates same-day after-school demand; IsolationForest flags unusual operating days for manager review.

Safety compiler

A state machine blocks unsafe reuse, requires cold-chain conditions, and keeps policy approval with cafeteria staff.

Action scorer

Recommendations balance expected recovery, CO2e, food value, labor minutes, stockout risk, confidence, and safety gates.

Impact receipt

Only approved primary marginal actions count, so totals cannot exceed recoverable returns, cooler capacity, or same-day demand.

Demo scenario

Rainy Tuesday runs through the whole stack.

The production demo uses a pasta lunch, juniors away on a field trip, a monitored 90-item cooler, and 175 after-school snack requests. The site animation mirrors the real workflow: feature vector -> model heads -> safety compiler -> manager-approved receipt.

Training

10,688 rows

Synthetic item-period cafeteria history across 540 school days.

Inference

Q10 / Q50 / Q90

Prediction intervals show uncertainty instead of pretending one number is exact.

Control

Human approval

The AI recommends actions; cafeteria staff approve food-safety and policy decisions.

Responsible AI

The AI recommends. Staff approve.

Second Bell does not decide whether food is safe or policy-approved to redistribute. Cold items require sealed packaging, monitored cooler capacity, and a 41 F-or-colder safety gate. The app uses synthetic data only and does not require student names, IDs, demographics, or individual eating behavior.
